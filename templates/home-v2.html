Frequently Asked Questions

NFC (Near Field Communication) is a wireless communication technology that allows devices to exchange data when they are in close proximity, typically within a few centimeters.

NFC works by establishing a magnetic field between two devices when they are close together. One device acts as the initiator and the other as a target, allowing data transfer between them.

Many modern smartphones, tablets, and some smartwatches come equipped with NFC capabilities. Additionally, specific NFC readers and writers are also available.

Yes, NFC is secure by design. Since NFC only works at very close range within about 4cm it’s hard for others to intercept the data making unauthorized access difficult.
